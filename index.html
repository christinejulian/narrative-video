RMS TITANIC
In the spring of 1912, a ship unlike any before it prepared for its maiden voyage—vast, luxurious, and hailed as unsinkable.
As Titanic left Southampton, more than 2,200 passengers and crew settled in, unaware that their journey would become one of history’s most haunting stories.
Four days later, under a moonless sky, the ship cut through calm waters. Then—an iceberg loomed ahead, silent and immense.
The impact was subtle, almost gentle. But beneath the surface, steel plates buckled. Water surged into the lower decks, sealing the ship’s fate.
Chaos spread as lifeboats were lowered into the freezing Atlantic. Amid the panic, the ship’s band played on—offering calm in the darkness.
At 2:20 a.m., Titanic slipped beneath the waves. Over 1,500 lives were lost. The disaster reshaped maritime safety forever, leaving a legacy written in sorrow and remembrance.
