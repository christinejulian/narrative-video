April 1912. The great ship glides across the North Atlantic—steel and dreams stitched together, certain of its own invincibility.
On her decks, laughter and quiet conversations drift beneath the smokestacks. Lifeboats hang silently, mere ornaments to a faith in progress.
Then—ice in the dark. The ship shudders. Orders barked, hearts racing. Lifeboats fill with faces caught between disbelief and terror.
In the days that follow, the sea keeps its secrets. Divers descend into the cold depths, searching for answers among twisted steel and silence.
